Backchecking system is in pressure mode

When do we backcheck?

We backcheck when we are in pressure mode (out of possession)

When the other team has the puck and is moving it towards our defensive zone, we initiate the backcheck.

  • Hard Inside Lanes: Skaters must sprint back through the middle dots to protect the high-danger royal road passing lanes.
  • Head on a Swivel: Identifying late-attaching trailer options moving up from the opposing blue line.
  • Stick on Stick Check: Disrupting the target's lower shaft or blade right as they attempt to settle a pass.

Why do we backcheck?

To prevent scoring opportunities and regain possession

The primary reason for initiating a backcheck is to limit the opposing team's ability to create scoring chances while working to recover the puck. A turnover is a high percentage scoring opportunity for us.

How We Do It?

  • Hard Work:All 5 players are responsible for backchecking with intensity and commitment.
  • With a plan:Skating with purpose and direction. Follow the backcheck plan.

Backcheck Plan

Responsibilities

Skate back hard and follow the plan below. If you do it will create good scoring chances for us the other way because the other team will be out of position.

  • D1:First player to cross the blue line. Keep the puck carrier to the outside, slow down the attack, and give teammates time to recover.
  • D2:Cover the most dangerous player without the puck. Take away the first passing option and protect the middle of the ice.
  • D3:If D1 has contained the puck carrier and D2 has removed the primary pass, attack the puck and help create a turnover.
  • D4:Take away the next passing option. Stay between your player and the net.
  • D5:Take away the final passing option and stay alert for late attackers entering the play.

Stop the rush, remove passing options, and force a turnover. Once the offensive team gains the zone, transition immediately into D-Zone Coverage.

4. Defensive Zone Coverage Integration

Transitioning Safely into Assigned Low-Zone Layouts

Smooth coordination between rushing backcheckers and set defensemen prevents coverage confusion as the play collapses deep inside the blue line.

  • Passing Off Coverage: Communicating transfers seamlessly so defensemen can protect the netfront while forwards lock down point threats.
  • Sorting the House: Filling the inner defensive box structure efficiently during fluid scramble situations.
