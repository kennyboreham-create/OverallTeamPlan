Zones

There are 3 zones on the ice. Offensive Zone, Neutral Zone, and Defensive Zone.

3 Types of Possession

There are 3 kinds of Possession. In Possession (Our team has the puck). Pressure mode (Out OF Possession and the other team has the puck). Battle mode (Loose/50-50 or no one has the puck, it is being bobbled, or it is 1 second after the other team has received a pass)

Strongside

The strong side is where the puck and majority of players are. The weakside is on the other side.

What is F1, F2, F3?

In the offensive zone. F1 is the deepest player in the zone. The F2 is the 2nd deepest skater in the zone. The 3 is the 3rd deepest in the zone.

What is D1, D2?

On the backcheck coming back to our zone. The first skater to cross the blue line is D1, second is D2, 3rd is D3, 4th is D4, and fifth is D5.

Forecheck

The forecheck is when the other team has possession in their end. We will play 1-2-2.

Strongside D

In the offensive zone, the strongside Defensemen will hold the line most of the time. The F3 will cover defensively.

Backcheck

The backcheck starts when the other team has possession and starts breaking out of their zone.

Breakouts

The breakout is when we have possession and are trying to bring the puck down the ice.

Regrouping

The phase where a team pulls back, resets its formation, and rebuilds its attack after moving the puck backward to maintain control.

Defensive Zone Coverage

We will run a box plus 1 in the defensive zone. Where the box stays tight to the net and the centre chases.

Pressure

Standard, Aggressive, Passive are the 3 types of pressure we will build in over the season. They will be associated with colours and will be posted on the boards when in effect.
