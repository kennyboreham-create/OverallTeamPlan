When to Attack

Good defenders don't just react, they look for chances to attack and win the puck. Attack when the other team loses control of the puck, bobbles it, or takes their eyes off it. A great time to pressure is during the first second after a player receives a pass, before they have control and can make a play. Make sure you are not the last man back. Smart pressure creates turnovers and helps our team get the puck back faster.

When to carry the puck, when to pass

Carry the puck when:

  • You have open ice in front of you.
  • No teammate is in a better position.
  • You can skate around a defender (not through).
  • You can create a scoring chance by attacking.

Pass the puck when:

  • A teammate has more space than you.
  • A defender is pressuring you.
  • The pass creates an odd-man rush (2-on-1, 3-on-2).
  • Your teammate has a better shooting opportunity.

Decision Making Steps

  1. 1 Can I attack open ice? → Carry
  2. 2 Is my teammate in a better position? → Pass
  3. 3 Neither? → Protect the puck and keep possession
